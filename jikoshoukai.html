Let's begin by presenting ourselves in japanese!

The japanese introduction, or ”じこしょうかい” (ji·ko·shō·ka·i) is a very important formality in Japan. You present yourself when you meet someone new, if you plan to continue seeing that person in the future (coworkers, school partners, neighbours, etc).

The formula we will use is (time of day) + Noun + は + Noun + です。どうぞよろしくおねがいします！。

Let's begin with how to say "good morning", "good day" or "good evening"!

To say "good morning" you could say "おはよう" (o·ha·yō) or, a little more formally, "おはようございます" (o·ha·yō·go·za·i·ma·su). The ō is an extension of the o, so the pronunciation will be something like "ohaioo". To say "good day" you say "こんにちは" (ko·n·ni·chi·wa). Note that we use the "ha" hiragana, but we pronounce it "wa". To say "good evening" you say "こんばんは" (ko·n·ba·n·wa). Again, note that "ha" is prononunced "wa".

Well done! We have the first part ready!

Then, we continue by actually introducing ourselves. The way to do this is to use the japanese word for "me" (わたし) + the particle は　+ our name　＋です。 So, I would say "わたし　は　ニコラス　です". Note that if you're not japanese, you have yo write your name in Katakana, and write it phonetically. In this example, my name, Nicholas, is transformed into Nikorasu.

Perfect! We have the second part ready!

Next, we could say a couple things about ourselves. Usually, you say your age, and where are you from, but you could say, for example, what you do for a living.

So, let's state our age!

Using the same formula, we say:　わたし　＋　は　＋ (our age) + さい　(the particle for "years old") + です。

And now, for where are we from, we again use the same formula, like this:

わたし　+　は　+　(our country, in katakana, unless you're from China, Korea or Japan!) + からきました.

Note that we repeat "わたしは"　in every sentence. We can omit it, or use it. It's up to you!

So, my complete じこしょうかい　would be:

こんにちは！　わたし　は　ニコラス　です。わたし　は　29さい　です。　アルゼンチン　から　きました。　どうぞ　よろしく　おねがいします！

And that's it! You now know how to introduce yourself in japanese!
